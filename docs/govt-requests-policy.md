# Government & Legal Data-Request Policy

_Last reviewed: 2026-07-15. This is the operative policy for handling requests from law enforcement, courts, or government authorities for user data. It answers the Meta App Review question on internal government-request policies._

## Policy

All requests from any public authority, law-enforcement body, court, or government agency for user personal data are reviewed personally by the founder / operator of UsefulMade before any disclosure.

1. **Valid legal process required.** We disclose user data only when compelled by a valid, legally binding order (subpoena, court order, or equivalent lawful demand under Indian law) that specifically identifies the data sought. We do not act on informal or voluntary requests.

2. **Data minimization.** We disclose only the minimum data the order legally compels — never more. We do not provide bulk access, standing access, or data outside the order's scope.

3. **Challenge improper requests.** We refuse or, where appropriate, legally challenge requests that are overbroad, vague, lack proper legal basis, or appear improper.

4. **User notification.** Where not legally prohibited, we notify the affected user before disclosure so they may seek to contest it.

5. **Logging.** Every request is recorded: date received, requesting authority, legal basis cited, the specific data disclosed (if any), and the date of response. This log is retained for accountability.

6. **Encryption note.** WhatsApp access tokens and payment-gateway secrets are stored encrypted (AES-256-GCM). We cannot produce plaintext credentials for data we do not hold in the clear.

## Scope

Applies to all Platform Data and user personal data held by the service (see `docs/meta-data-handling.md` for the full data map).
